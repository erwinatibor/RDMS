Who We Are

Rwanda's Oral–Systemic Health Movement.

Dento-Medical Society Rwanda (RDMS) is a registered non-profit founded on 1 July 2024 in Huye, Rwanda. We unite dental surgeons, dental therapists, and public health professionals around one shared conviction: oral health is not separate from general health.

Founded by Igisubizo Jimmy Confiance and registered under the Rwanda Development Board (RDB Code: 143885158), RDMS delivers free mobile dental clinics, professional education, research programmes, and policy advocacy for Rwanda's most underserved communities.

What We Do

Six Ways We're Changing Oral Health in Rwanda.

From free mobile clinics in rural areas to policy advocacy at the national level — RDMS delivers integrated oral health solutions across Rwanda's healthcare landscape.

Community Mobile Clinics

Free and subsidized preventive and curative dental services — examinations, cleanings, fluoride, extractions, and referrals — delivered directly to rural and underserved communities.

School-Based Programs

Dental hygiene education in Rwandan schools teaching children correct brushing technique, impact of diet on teeth, and when to seek care — building lifelong healthy habits.

Interdisciplinary Workshops

Training sessions that bring dental and general healthcare professionals together to advance integrated care and share knowledge on oral-systemic health connections.

Research Collaborations

Partnerships with academic institutions nationally and internationally to advance evidence-based oral-systemic health research and publish via the Dental Medicine Chronicles.

Public Health Campaigns

Oral hygiene awareness and preventive care campaigns for schools and communities in English, Kinyarwanda, and French — covering gum disease, decay prevention, and systemic health.

Policy Advocacy

Policy dialogues with government at district, provincial, and national level to integrate dentistry into Rwanda's national health agenda and improve healthcare legislation.

Why It Matters

Dentistry is Underfunded. Rwanda Pays the Price.

Across Rwanda, oral healthcare is consistently underfunded, understaffed, and overlooked. Fragmented service delivery, low public awareness, and a shortage of preventive care leave millions without access to even basic dental attention.

The consequences extend far beyond the mouth. Untreated gum disease is linked to heart disease, diabetes, premature birth, and respiratory illness. These are preventable outcomes — and RDMS exists to prevent them.

The Oral–Systemic Connection

Heart Disease

Gum disease bacteria enter the bloodstream, contributing to cardiovascular inflammation

Diabetes

Periodontitis worsens blood sugar control — and diabetes accelerates gum disease

Premature Birth

Untreated gum infections during pregnancy are linked to preterm birth and low birth weight

Respiratory Disease

Oral bacteria aspirated into the lungs can trigger or worsen pneumonia and related conditions
